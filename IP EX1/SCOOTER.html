    FEATURES:

  1. A scooter is a motorcycle with an underbone or step-through frame, a seat, and a platform for the rider's feet, emphasizing comfort and fuel economy.
  2. Elements of scooter design were present in some of the earliest motorcycles, and motor scooters have been made since at least 1914.
  3. The global popularity of motor scooters dates from the post-World War II introductions of the Vespa and Lambretta models in Italy.
  4. Motor scooters are very popular in Asia, particularly in countries such as India, Indonesia, Thailand, Vietnam, China, Japan and Taiwan where there is local manufacturing.
  5. Scooters may be powered by an electric motor powered by a rechargeable battery.
  6. Electric scooters are rising in popularity because of higher gasoline prices, and battery technology is gradually improving, making this form of transportation more practical.
  7. Scooters are popular for personal transportation partly due to being more affordable, easier to operate, and more convenient to park and store than a car.
  8. Licensing requirements for scooters are easier and cheaper than for cars in most parts of the world, and insurance is usually cheaper.
  9. Some scooters, including the BMW C1 and the Honda Gyro Canopy, have a windscreen and a roof. The Piaggio MP3 offered a tall windscreen with roof as an option.
  10. High-end scooter models now include comprehensive technological features, including cast aluminium frames, engines with integral counterbalancing, and cross-linked brake systems.
